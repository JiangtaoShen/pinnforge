# b00 — control: baseline measured as-is (setup block, not a subagent)

## Thrust & rationale
b00 is the setup/control node, not a block product: it measures
`task/baseline.py` (the vanilla PINN tree root) exactly as shipped, to
fix the rRMSE every later block must beat. No fork — this *is* the root.

The baseline is a deliberately barebones vanilla PINN for the
KS-chaotic task (`t ≤ 0.4` window):
- single plain MLP, 5×100, tanh everywhere, He-uniform init, scalar
  output `u`; **no** feature mapping, Fourier embedding, skip
  connections, or special activations (all left for blocks to discover);
- soft losses at equal weight: PDE residual (KS `u_t + V1 u u_x +
  V2 u_xx + V3 u_xxxx`, 4th-order term by nested autograd) + IC
  supervision at `t=0` (from `ks_ic.csv`) + periodic-BC penalty
  `u(t,0)=u(t,2π)`;
- fixed LR 1e-3 Adam, no schedule; uniform-grid collocation, minibatched
  (1000 col / 128 IC / 128 BC);
- wall-deadline training: whole process ≤ `TRAIN_TIME = 300 s`.

## Findings
The vanilla PINN partially reconstructs the KS field but is far from
accurate — rRMSE **0.730** at ~50k Adam steps in 300 s. Large headroom:
the 4th-order `u_xxxx` residual is stiff and its nested-`jacfwd`
evaluation dominates per-step cost, so a plain tanh MLP at fixed LR
under-resolves the chaotic spatial structure. Open directions for blocks
(consult kb1): Fourier/random-feature embeddings for the higher spatial
wavenumbers, hard periodic BC, stronger optimizers/preconditioning
(SOAP), LR schedules, causal/time-marching weighting for the chaotic
trajectory, and cheaper high-order-derivative evaluation.
